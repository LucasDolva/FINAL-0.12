Our Accessibility design decisions

From Typography too color contrast, in this page we discuss our attemts to make our blog as accsessible as possible.

Introduction

Below we explain our accessibility choices. The steps are in importance order, so we tried to show our thought process through theese steps below, where number 1 is most important and so on. We recognize that every step is as important as the other, but this is how we went through our accessibility design.

We tried to design our page with accessibility in mind from the get go, and below we wil discuss the changes we made after checking accessibility through a WCAGG checker.

01

Typography and colors

Our good teacher Carlos taught us that good color contrast is key for accessebility. So every color that is chosen on our page is checked for contrast throgh Coolors contrast checker

In Addition our typeface "rubik" was chosen due to its more playful circular style with a falback typeface of sans-serif. We made sure all text had excelent color contrast, and was big enough for me to se without glasses!

02

Consistency in design

We know a person with visual disibility and actually consoulted her for feedback on what is important. For starters

  • Accessibility in mind from the get go
  • all images have alt text
  • the NAV is easily identifiable, and consistent throught-out the page
  • page is manouverable with a keybord, and your eyes closed

03

Content and performance

For Content, we made sure that no essential content is conveyed via images. All essential content is either a strong or a em tag.

For performance we the Squoosh app in order to compress and resize needlessly big images, so that the page may runn faster. We acctually saw a significant performance enhancment due to this big effort.
